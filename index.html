IQ Test

An Intelligence Quotient (IQ) test is a standardized assessment designed to measure human intelligence relative to a population. In other words, the term
"intelligence quotient" (IQ) refers to a test that evaluates a variety of cognitive skills and yields a result that is meant to be a gauge of a person's
intellectual capacity. IQ tests have become a widely accepted tool for assessing cognitive abilities, problem-solving skills, and mental reasoning.

IQ tests are based on the principle that intelligence can be quantified and expressed as a single number that reflects cognitive abilities. The average score on
an IQ test is set at 100, with most of the population scoring within a range of 85 to 115. Scores above 130 are generally considered to indicate high
intelligence, while scores below 70 may signal cognitive challenges.

The questions in an IQ test typically cover a variety of areas, including numerical reasoning, logical reasoning, verbal intelligence, and spatial intelligence.
Numerical reasoning tests an individual's ability to work with numbers and solve mathematical problems. Logical reasoning assesses pattern recognition
and the ability to make sense of complex sequences. Verbal intelligence focuses on understanding language, word relationships, and analogies.
Spatial intelligence involves the ability to visualize and manipulate objects in space, which is important for activities like reading maps or solving puzzles..
